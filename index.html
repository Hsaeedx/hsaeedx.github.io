About Me

Physician-in-training pursuing Otolaryngology, with a CS engineering degree and a track record of building things that matter.

I'm a third-year medical student at The Ohio State University College of Medicine with a B.S. in Computer Science Engineering from the University of Michigan. My work lives at the crossroads of clinical medicine and technology.

As a researcher at OSU's Department of Otolaryngology, I lead Medicare Claims data analysis as project leader and primary data scientist.

I am also a Machine Learning Engineer at Michigan Medicine's Frankel Cardiovascular Center, developing novel ML models for atrial flutter detection in 12-lead ECGs in collaboration with Michigan Medicine and General Electric.

I founded HUJA, LLC, developing AI Explain — an Anki add-on that generates high-yield, AI-powered medical flashcard explanations actively used by medical students preparing for USMLE board exams.

I also founded CliniCorps, a Michigan registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit — our near-term goal is to deploy free, open-source EMR software to local free clinics.

Projects

Things I've built, researched, and founded — spanning clinical AI, healthcare access, and medical education.

Department of Otolaryngology · The Ohio State University

CMS Medicare Claims Research

As project leader and primary data scientist in the OSU Department of Otolaryngology, I analyze large-scale CMS Medicare FFS claims data to drive improvements in ENT patient care nationally.

TORS vs. Radiation for Oropharyngeal Squamous Cell Carcinoma
Propensity-matched comparison of transoral robotic surgery vs. radiation-based therapy for non-metastatic OPSCC — evaluating survival and functional outcomes including dysphagia, gastrostomy tube, and tracheostomy rates.
Early vs. Late Speech-Language Pathology After Acute Stroke
Impact of outpatient SLP referral timing on aspiration pneumonia, dysphagia diagnoses, and G-tube placement at 1 year post-discharge among Medicare stroke patients.
Aggressiveness of End-of-Life Care in Head & Neck Cancer Patients on Immune Checkpoint Inhibitors
Characterizing end-of-life care patterns among Medicare beneficiaries with head and neck cancer who received immune checkpoint inhibitors, including hospice utilization and late-stage intervention rates.

Division of Cardiovascular Medicine · University of Michigan

Atrial Flutter Detection Algorithm

At the University of Michigan, I am developing a novel ML model for atrial flutter detection in 12-lead ECGs that substantially outperforms commercial algorithms in recall — a critical metric when missing a diagnosis has serious consequences. The work is conducted in collaboration with Michigan Medicine and General Electric, building a large-scale ECG repository for ongoing electrophysiology research.
